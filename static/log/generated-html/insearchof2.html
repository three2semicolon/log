RAVE:N, The Remixes

screencap from the music video for JD. REID and Shygirl's remix of Holier, dir. Yasser Abubeker (courtesy of sofiaswave on Tumblr)

the music and aesthetics surrounding Kelela's 2023 album Raven is, to me, perfect representation afrofuturism. it's an emotional alternative r&b album drenched in electronic music that explores the delicate push and pull constant throughout all aspects of our lives (but especially on the dance floor).

i've lived in new york city for about a year now (may 2025 - 2026), and within those 365 days i've heard this album in the background of countless black and queer cafes, record stores, bars, and vintage shops.

courtesy of Teen Vogue

beautiful writeup about this album, it's themes, its relevance, and the feelings it invokes from John Wholmacher via beatsperminute.com

RAVE:N is a remix album that recontextualizes kelela's performances in a way that feels like a true companion piece to the original album. it exists as an extension of the original, an expansion of the auditory and visual universe that is Raven.

the art of the remix is underrated, yet vital. a core piece of musical history, foundational to the genre's our ears call home today. where would we be without the dj?

this is a project conceived for and by the dj. highly recommend caniholdthamic's article on their experience at the 7/25/24 RAVE:N live performance and surrounding dj sets. i am forever jealous.

"I’m so freed by the idea that this will have many different lives. I’m just coming up with the original tapestry."

getting started

in this edition of insearchof, i'm exploring the artists kelela brought into her Raven universe by way of this remix album. it's a celebration of black music (in and out of the club), full of established and underground producers, djs, and vocalists.

this is not comprehensive, there are so many artists on this thing. i'll be highlighting one or two of my favorite songs from each artist discussed. please check out the respective artist's soundclouds and bandcamps. so many gems, a lot of them not available on traditional streaming platforms.

closing notes

this is a bit of a departure from my original intention for in search of; rather than a weave of artists contributing throughout a single cohesive album (see vol. 1), this post is centered around a remix album where kelela has handpicked artists to take full control for each song. very different approaches, both very fun to cover, both extremely meaningful.

makes me consider what this series will be in the future.

I've been wanting to de-algorithm myself. i want reclaim my time and control over what i listen to. in this context it means finding new music through other artists (collaborations, dj sets, reposts, likes, curated playlists, articles) rather than trusting spotify's algorithm to suggest me good stuff.

this doesn't mean the algorithm is incapable of getting great music in your ears (i've found spotify actually does this pretty well), but rather that in an era of AI and an era where human connection is hard to come by (by design) we need to fight back. reclaim your consumption.

soundcloud, nfs radio, soulseek

thesis: build your tastes through other humans, trust artists over the robots. find and cultivate community

working on that myself,
